CARDAMOM

Green cardamom is one of the most expensive spices by weight, but little is needed to impart flavor. It is best stored in the pod, as exposed or ground seeds quickly lose their flavor. Grinding the pods and seeds together lowers both the quality and the price.

Black Pepper

Black pepper is the world's most traded spice, and is one of the most common spices added to cuisines around the world. Its spiciness is due to the chemical compound piperine, which is a different kind of spiciness from that of capsaicin characteristic of chili peppers.

Cloves

Cloves, the dried flower buds of the Syzygium aromaticum tree, are a popular spice known for their strong, aromatic flavor and various culinary and medicinal uses.

Coffee

Coffee bean powder, also known as ground coffee, is the finely milled form of roasted coffee beans used to brew the popular beverage, and its fineness impacts the brewing process and the resulting coffee's flavor.
